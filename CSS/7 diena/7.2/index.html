FLAMINGO

They are the only birds that feed by filtering water. After immersing their beaks in water, flamingos use their tongues to drive water into their beaks and cough it up through slits in their beaks, collecting small plants and animals.

  • Scientific name: Phoenicopteridae
  • Height: 90-1,5 m
  • Mass: 1,2-4 kg
  • Lifespan: up to 60 years

More than one species of flamingo lives in the world. They are common in South America, Africa, the Middle East, and the Mediterranean region. Not all flamingos are this bright pink. Some are red, some are almost white.
